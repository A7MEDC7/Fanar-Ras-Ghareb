History Of R'as Ghareb

The city of Ras Ghareb was given this name in relation to Mount Ghareb, which overlooks the city from the west and has a height of 1750 meters. As for the groin in the camel, it is “the area between the hump and the neck.” There was only the lighthouse in Ras Ghareb, which was established in 1871 AD and was designed by the French Gustave Eiffel, who erected the tower known by his name as the Eiffel Tower, as well as the Abu El-Ela Bridge in Cairo.
